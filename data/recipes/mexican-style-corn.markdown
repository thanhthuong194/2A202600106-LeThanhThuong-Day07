# Mexican-Style Corn

## Grilled Corn (Elote-Style)

Based on [NYT Grilled Corn Mexican Style](https://cooking.nytimes.com/recipes/1013216-grilled-corn-mexican-style) by Mark Bittman

### Ingredients

- 4 ears of corn, husked
- 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
- 1-2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
- 1/4 teaspoon chili powder (Lime Tajín or cayenne also work)
- salt
- freshly ground black pepper
- queso fresco, for topping (optional)

### Directions

1. Grill corn over medium-high heat until kernels begin to char, about 5 minutes. Turn and continue cooking until all sides are slightly blackened.
2. Mix mayonnaise, lime juice, chili powder, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Adjust to taste.
3. Serve corn with chili-lime mayo, topped with queso fresco if desired.

### Variations

- Add fresh peaches when in season - wonderful summer treat.

## Charred and Raw Corn Salad

Based on [Bon Appétit Charred and Raw Corn with Chile and Cheese](http://www.bonappetit.com/recipe/charred-and-raw-corn-with-chile-and-cheese) by Alison Roman

### Ingredients

- 6 ears of corn, kernels cut from cobs
- 4 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, divided
- 1 small shallot, cut into rings
- 1 red chile (Fresno or red jalapeño), sliced into rings
- 5 Tbsp fresh lime juice
- kosher salt
- 3 Tbsp whole-milk Greek yogurt
- 1 oz finely grated Parmesan (about 3 Tbsp)
- 1/2 cup cilantro leaves, coarsely chopped

### Directions

1. Heat 1 Tbsp oil in a cast iron skillet over medium-high. Add one-third of corn kernels and cook undisturbed until charred underneath, 1-2 minutes. Toss and continue cooking until charred all over, 2-3 minutes more.
2. Meanwhile, mix shallot, chile, lime juice, and remaining 3 Tbsp oil in a bowl; season with salt. Let sit at least 5 minutes.
3. Add charred corn, remaining raw corn kernels, yogurt, Parmesan, and half of cilantro; season with salt and toss.
4. Top with more Parmesan and remaining cilantro before serving.

### Notes

- Made with red bell peppers instead of chile - quite good. Could add an additional jalapeño for more heat.
- Some commenters prefer to char all the corn for a more elote-like dish.
