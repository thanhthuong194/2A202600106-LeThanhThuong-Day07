# Carnitas

Based on [Serious Eats No-Waste Tacos de Carnitas](https://www.seriouseats.com/no-waste-tacos-de-carnitas-with-salsa-verde-recipe) by J. Kenji López-Alt

## Ingredients

### For the Carnitas

- 3 pounds boneless pork shoulder, cut into 2-inch cubes
- 1 tablespoon kosher salt
- 2 medium onions, divided (one diced for serving, one quartered for cooking)
- 1 medium orange, quartered
- 6 cloves garlic, halved (4 for cooking, 2 for salsa)
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 cinnamon stick, broken into pieces
- 1/4 cup vegetable oil
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro

### For the Salsa Verde

- 6 medium tomatillos, husked and halved
- 2 jalapeño peppers, stemmed and halved
- Reserved cooking liquid from pork

### For Serving

- 24 corn tortillas
- 1 cup crumbled queso fresco
- 3 limes, cut into wedges

## Directions

1. Preheat oven to 275°F. Season pork with salt and pack tightly into a 9x13 glass baking dish. Squeeze orange juice over pork, then nestle orange pieces, 2 onion quarters, 4 garlic cloves, bay leaves, and cinnamon into dish. Pour vegetable oil over surface.
2. Cover tightly with foil and bake until fork tender, about 3.5 hours.
3. Strain pork, reserving liquid and fat separately. Discard orange, onion, garlic, bay, cinnamon. Return fat to pork, shred into large chunks.
4. For salsa: Add tomatillos, remaining onion quarters, remaining garlic, and jalapeños to reserved cooking liquid. Add water to almost cover. Boil, then simmer 10 minutes until tender. Blend until smooth, season with salt.
5. To crisp: Broil pork 4 inches from heat for 6 minutes, stir, broil 6 more minutes.
6. Serve in doubled corn tortillas with salsa verde, diced onion/cilantro, queso fresco, and lime.

## Notes

- Made twice, both times really good. First was actually better (saltier?).
- Large cast-iron skillet is a good approach.
- 6 lb of meat was not enough for 17 people - would have doubled.
- Added Mexican oregano the second time.
- Possible to overcook - stick to under 4 hours.
- Can prepare through step 3 up to 3 days ahead; crisp from fridge.
- Freezes well - freeze flat for easy broiling from frozen.
