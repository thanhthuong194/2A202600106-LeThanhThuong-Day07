# Pasta with Parsnips and Bacon

Based on [NYT Cooking Pasta With Parsnips and Bacon](https://cooking.nytimes.com/recipes/1015979-pasta-with-parsnips-and-bacon) by Melissa Clark

## Ingredients

- 3 medium parsnips (3/4 pound), peeled and cut into 1/2-inch pieces
- 1 1/2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
- kosher salt and black pepper
- 1/2 pound dried campanelle or farfalle pasta
- 1/4 pound bacon, diced
- 1 medium leek, thinly sliced
- 3/4 cup heavy cream
- 2/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 2 tablespoons chopped parsley

## Directions

1. Heat oven to 400°F. Toss parsnips with oil and season with salt and pepper. Roast, tossing occasionally, until golden and tender, about 25 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta according to package instructions until 1 minute before al dente. Drain.
3. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, brown the bacon until crisp, about 5 minutes. Transfer bacon to a paper-towel-lined plate. Discard all but 1 tablespoon of fat.
4. Return skillet to heat and add leeks. Cook in remaining bacon fat until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in heavy cream and cooked bacon. Simmer until slightly thickened, 2-3 minutes.
5. Stir in pasta, parsnips, and cheese. Simmer until heated through and cheese is melted. Season with salt and pepper; toss with parsley. Serve drizzled with olive oil and plenty of black pepper.

## Notes

- Cook parsnips longer than you think - they should be golden and slightly crunchy. Consider broiling at the end to get more color and heat them up before combining.
- Serves 2 as a main, 45 minutes total.
