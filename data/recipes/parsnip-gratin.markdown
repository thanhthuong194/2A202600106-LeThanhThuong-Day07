# Parsnip Gratin

Based on [Food52 Parsnip Gratin with Melty Gruyère and Thyme](https://food52.com/recipes/19693-parsnip-gratin-with-melty-gruyere-and-thyme)

## Ingredients

- 2 1/4 pounds parsnips, peeled
- 1 onion, thinly sliced
- 3 cups heavy cream
- 1 teaspoon butter (for greasing)
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg, plus more for topping
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/4 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tablespoon flour (or rice flour for gluten-free)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 6 ounces gruyère, grated

## Directions

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Using a mandoline, slice parsnips to 1/8-inch thin slices. The thinner the better.
2. Generously grease a 6x9-inch baking dish with butter.
3. Layer parsnips and onion with half the cheese and half the thyme, reserving the bigger parsnip pieces for the final layer. Press down and make layers even. Arrange the top layer in nice overlapping rows.
4. In a small pot, heat cream. Whisk in salt, nutmeg, remaining thyme, white pepper, flour, and garlic. When it just comes to a simmer, pour over the layered parsnips, tilting dish to get cream into all the nooks.
5. Sprinkle remaining cheese over top, a few more thyme leaves, and a little nutmeg.
6. Cover securely with foil and bake 50 minutes on middle rack.
7. Remove foil and pierce with fork - parsnips should be al dente. Bake another 15 minutes uncovered until golden and bubbly.
8. Let sit at room temperature 15-20 minutes before serving so it sets and thickens.
